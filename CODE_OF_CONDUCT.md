# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences**
- **Gracefully accepting constructive criticism**
- **Focusing on what is best for the community**
- **Showing empathy towards other community members**
- **Providing evidence-based arguments**
- **Respecting privacy and security boundaries**
- **Collaborating on research and investigations**
- **Sharing knowledge and resources**
- **Following ethical research practices**

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **The use of sexualized language or imagery**
- **Unwelcome sexual attention or advances**
- **Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments**
- **Personal or political attacks**
- **Public or private harassment**
- **Publishing others' private information without explicit permission**
- **Threats of violence or harm**
- **Discrimination based on protected characteristics**
- **Spam, advertising, or self-promotion without relevance**
- **Deliberate misinformation or disinformation**
- **Violating legal or ethical boundaries**
- **Endangering other researchers through reckless disclosure**

## Research-Specific Guidelines

### 🔍 Evidence-Based Research

- **Require Verification**: All claims must be supported by verifiable evidence
- **Document Sources**: Clearly cite all sources and references
- **Avoid Speculation**: Distinguish between facts and theories
- **Update Information**: Correct errors promptly and transparently
- **Peer Review**: Encourage community verification of findings

### 🛡️ Security and Privacy

- **Protect Identities**: Respect privacy of individuals mentioned in research
- **Secure Communications**: Use encrypted channels for sensitive discussions
- **Responsible Disclosure**: Consider implications of public disclosures
- **Operational Security**: Maintain good security practices
- **Legal Compliance**: Follow applicable laws and regulations

### ⚖️ Ethical Considerations

- **Informed Consent**: Obtain consent when profiling individuals
- **Minimize Harm**: Consider potential negative impacts of research
- **Transparency**: Be clear about methodologies and limitations
- **Accountability**: Take responsibility for research outcomes
- **Professional Conduct**: Maintain high standards of integrity

## Enforcement

### Reporting Guidelines

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it:

**How to Report:**
1. **Private Message**: Contact maintainers directly through secure channels
2. **GitHub Issues**: Create a private issue with "conduct" label
3. **Email**: Send to project maintainers (use encrypted email when possible)

**What to Include:**
- Description of the incident
- Date and time of occurrence
- Individuals involved
- Any evidence or witnesses
- Desired resolution

### Investigation Process

1. **Immediate Response**: Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. **Preliminary Review**: Initial assessment within 3 days
3. **Full Investigation**: Detailed review within 7 days
4. **Resolution**: Appropriate action and follow-up
5. **Documentation**: Record handling for future reference

### Consequences

Unacceptable behavior may result in:

- **Warning**: Private or public warning about behavior
- **Temporary Suspension**: Restricted access for specified period
- **Permanent Ban**: Removal from project and community spaces
- **Legal Action**: Reporting to authorities for serious violations
- **Public Disclosure**: Notification to community about serious breaches

## Conflict Resolution

### Disagreement Guidelines

When disagreements arise:

1. **Assume Good Faith**: Believe others intend to be constructive
2. **Focus on Issues**: Address problems, not personalities
3. **Seek Understanding**: Ask clarifying questions before responding
4. **Find Common Ground**: Look for shared goals and values
5. **Escalate When Needed**: Request mediator assistance if stuck

### Mediation Process

For serious conflicts:

1. **Request Mediation**: Contact maintainers for neutral third-party help
2. **All Parties Involved**: Ensure all voices are heard
3. **Document Process**: Keep records of discussions and agreements
4. **Follow Decisions**: Accept and implement agreed resolutions
5. **Learn and Improve**: Use conflicts as opportunities for growth

## Community Expectations

### For Contributors

- **Quality Standards**: Maintain high standards for research and documentation
- **Collaboration**: Work cooperatively with other researchers
- **Mentorship**: Help newcomers learn and contribute effectively
- **Feedback**: Provide constructive feedback to others
- **Continuous Learning**: Stay updated on best practices and methodologies

### For Maintainers

- **Fair Treatment**: Apply standards consistently to all community members
- **Transparency**: Be open about decisions and processes
- **Accessibility**: Ensure community resources are accessible to all
- **Support**: Provide help and guidance to contributors
- **Protection**: Safeguard community members from harassment

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- **All Project Spaces**: GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions
- **Public Spaces**: Social media, conferences, events related to the project
- **Private Communications**: Direct messages, emails, and other private interactions
- **Offline Activities**: In-person meetings and events related to the project
